What is DSOC?

- AND WHY DOES IT MATTER? -

DSOC stands for Deep Space Optical Communications, and it is NASA’s first demonstration of laser communications from deep space, covering Earth-Mars distances. This technology has the potential to enable higher data-rate returns from future robotic and human missions to Mars and beyond, as well as reduce the size, weight, and power requirements of spacecraft communication systems.
DSOC is a technology demonstration, which means it will test key technologies that may be used in future missions. DSOC will operate for about two years, beginning roughly 20 days after the launch of Psyche in October 2023.

Get to Know Us

We are an ambitious team of Arizona State University undergrads, involved in NASA's Psyche Project, part of the Tungsten Class. We are developing a detector for amateur telescopes to detect the laser from the Psyche DSOC instrument. Our diverse team of engineers, physicists, and astronomy fans aim to create a cost-effective and user-friendly solution to enable amateur astronomers and space enthusiasts to contribute to the Psyche project.
